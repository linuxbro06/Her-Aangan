      Overview

      1. Basics of Periods

        What is it? Menstruation is the shedding of the uterine lining, which happens monthly as part of the menstrual cycle. Duration: Typically lasts 3–7 days. Cycle Length: A full menstrual cycle is usually 21–35 days but can vary.


      2. Symptoms

        Physical Symptoms: Cramps, bloating, breast tenderness, fatigue, headaches. Emotional Symptoms: Mood swings, irritability, or feeling emotional (PMS). Spotting or heavy flow: Both are normal in early periods but seek help if either is extreme.


      3. Managing Periods

        Hygiene Products: Pads: Best for beginners; change every 4–6 hours. Tampons: Insertable; change every 4–8 hours to prevent Toxic Shock Syndrome (TSS). Menstrual Cups: Reusable and eco-friendly; empty every 8–12 hours. Period Underwear: Absorbent and convenient for light to moderate flow. Bathing: Stay clean and fresh with daily showers. Hygiene: Always wipe front to back to prevent infections.


      4. Dealing with Cramps

        Use a hot water bottle or heating pad on the abdomen. Take over-the-counter pain relievers (e.g., ibuprofen) if needed. Engage in light exercise like yoga or stretching. Stay hydrated and eat magnesium-rich foods like bananas and spinach.


      5. Emotional Well-being

        Understand it’s normal to feel emotional or irritable. Journaling or talking to someone can help. Practice relaxation techniques like deep breathing or meditation.


      6. When to Seek Help

        Periods are excessively painful or heavy. Irregular cycles after the first 1–2 years. Skipping periods (if not on birth control and not sexually active). Signs of infection: unusual smell, itching, or pain.


      7. Tips for School or Work

        Keep a small pouch with pads, tampons, or cups. Pack extra underwear and a zip-lock bag for emergencies. Stay prepared with pain relief and snacks for low energy.


      8. FAQs

        Is it normal to have irregular periods at first? Yes, cycles can take up to 2 years to regulate. Can I swim on my period? Absolutely, with tampons or menstrual cups. What should I avoid eating? There’s no strict rule, but limiting caffeine and salty foods can reduce bloating.


      9. Empowerment and Positivity

        Periods are a natural and healthy sign of growing up. Talk openly with trusted adults or friends. Embrace period positivity—every girl experiences it! Would you like detailed advice on any specific aspect?
